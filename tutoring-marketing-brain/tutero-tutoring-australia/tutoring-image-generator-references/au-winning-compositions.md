# AU Winning Compositions

Six prompt templates, each derived from a proven winning ad in the Tutero AU Meta library. These are not creative suggestions — they are reverse-engineered from creatives that converted real Australian parents.

For every template:
- The **Source** line names which winner(s) it's based on (cross-reference `paid-social-winner-insights-australia`).
- The **Winning move** line names which of the seven moves it executes.
- Fill in `[bracketed]` variables.
- Append the universal anti-AI-glow language from `aesthetic-rules.md` to every prompt.

## Composition 1 — Whole Transaction in One Frame

**Source:** Winners 5, 6, 7
**Winning moves:** 2 (faces), 5 (Australianness), 6 (whole transaction in one frame)
**Mode:** Warm Staged
**Best for:** Cold-audience Meta feed ads
**Aspect ratio:** 4:5 or 1:1

The single highest-leverage frame in AU paid social. It contains every element of the transaction in one image: the parent, the child, the tutor (visible on the laptop screen), the laptop itself, and an unmistakably Australian setting. The parent's mental work of "imagining how this works" is done for them.

```
Photograph of an Australian [parent persona — e.g. mum in her late 30s wearing a
casual t-shirt] and her [child age and gender — e.g. 9-year-old son in a blue
school polo] sitting side by side at a [setting — e.g. kitchen counter / dining
table / family room desk] in a typical Australian suburban home. An open silver
laptop sits in front of them on the [counter/table]. On the laptop screen, a
friendly [tutor description — e.g. female tutor in her late 20s] is visible
mid-lesson via video call, slightly pixelated webcam quality, the tutor smiling
warmly. The [parent] has [her/his] arm casually around the child's shoulder.
Both the parent and child are looking directly at the camera with natural,
genuine smiles — [optional: giving a thumbs up / waving at the laptop / one
giving a thumbs up]. The kitchen behind them shows [AU details — white cabinets,
tiled splashback, fridge with kid drawings, a fruit bowl]. Flat overhead kitchen
lighting, neutral white balance, late morning or early afternoon weekday feel.
Looks like an iPhone photo a Tutero staff member took during a real session.
```

**Then append the negative block** (see `aesthetic-rules.md`).

**Variants to test:**
- Mum + primary boy (Winner 6 archetype)
- Mum + teen boy (less common, higher persona-specificity)
- Dad + child (rarest, breaks pattern)
- Sibling pair both at the laptop (signals "more than one kid")

## Composition 2 — Tutor Headshot Grid

**Source:** Winners 2, 10, 12
**Winning moves:** 1 (year-range works on the headline overlay), 2 (faces × N), 5 (Australianness via diverse-AU names)
**Mode:** Warm Staged
**Best for:** Cold-audience static ads, top-of-funnel
**Aspect ratio:** 1:1

Density of faces is itself a persuasion lever. Each headshot must be generated separately at 1:1, then composited into a grid via code (HTML/SVG overlay) — do NOT try to make Nano Banana generate the grid in one shot, the faces will be inconsistent and warped.

**Generate ONE headshot at a time using this template:**

```
Photograph of [tutor name — e.g. Sarah, Faraz, Zhang, Joey, Freya, Sethumi,
Priya, Rose] — a [tutor description — e.g. friendly Australian woman in her
late 20s with [specific hair detail], wearing a [casual top — olive green
t-shirt / soft blue jumper / cream button-up]]. Head and shoulders shot, looking
directly at the camera with a warm, genuine, closed-mouth or small open smile.
Plain neutral background — soft cream, light grey, or pale sage green wall.
Flat soft daylight from a large window slightly off camera, no harsh shadows.
Natural skin texture, visible pores, no retouching. Looks like an honest
professional photo taken on a phone, NOT a corporate headshot, NOT a fashion
portrait. Subject framed centre, eyes in upper third.
```

**Then append the negative block.**

**Critical for the grid:**
- Generate 8 or 12 headshots (Winner 2 = 8, Winner 12 = 12)
- Mix ages (mostly 20s–30s, one or two 40s for "experienced")
- Mix genders (roughly 60/40 female/male — reflects the actual tutor base)
- Mix ethnicities naturally — Anglo-Australian, Lebanese-Australian, Chinese-Australian, Indian-Australian, Greek-Australian, Pacific Islander-Australian. Describe individuals, never "diverse group".
- Use the SAME background colour family across all headshots so the grid feels cohesive
- Use the SAME lighting direction across all headshots
- Each headshot should be paired with a first name + one-line personality descriptor in the overlay (e.g. "Sarah — a patient maths tutor who loves teaching primary kids")

The grid itself is then composited in HTML/SVG with:
- 4×2 grid (8 tutors) for standard density
- 6×2 grid (12 tutors) for maximum density variant
- White or pale background between cells
- "Online Tutoring for Prep–Year 12" headline above the grid in Tutero brand colours
- Optional small "qualified tutors" subhead below

## Composition 3 — School Gate Hug

**Source:** Winner 9
**Winning moves:** 4 (outcome substitution — guilt relief), 5 (Australianness via uniform + school gate), 7 (persona specificity — working mum)
**Mode:** Warm Staged
**Best for:** Working-mum persona, retargeting working-parent audiences
**Aspect ratio:** 4:5 or 1:1

The single highest-emotion AU frame. Sells guilt relief, not tutoring. The school gate itself is the moment working-parent guilt peaks.

```
Photograph of an Australian working mum in her late 30s or early 40s, wearing
[work clothes — navy blazer, smart casual shirt, professional dress], crouching
or leaning down to hug her [age]-year-old daughter who is wearing a typical
Australian primary school uniform (white polo shirt with school logo, navy or
green checked summer dress, black school shoes, school bag on her back).
They are at the school gate of a typical Australian public primary school —
red brick or cream building visible behind them, "Public School" signage softly
visible, other parents and kids walking past in the background slightly out of
focus. The mum is smiling warmly down at her daughter; the daughter is smiling
back up. Mid-morning or mid-afternoon flat Australian daylight, slightly overcast
or harsh midday sun softened by tree shade. Natural skin texture, no retouching.
Looks like a candid phone snap a friend took, posted to Instagram with no filter.
Aspect: vertical, focal point centre-right, left third available for headline
overlay.
```

**Then append the negative block.**

**Variant:** Replace daughter with primary-school son. Replace the smile with a quick wave goodbye for a different emotional beat.

## Composition 4 — Testimonial Portrait

**Source:** Winner 3
**Winning moves:** 2 (face), 4 (outcome substitution — confidence + screen time), 7 (persona specificity)
**Mode:** Warm Staged for the smile, Candid Real for the setting
**Best for:** Retargeting, social proof, mid-funnel
**Aspect ratio:** 4:5 (portrait) or 1:1

A single mum looking calmly and warmly at the camera. The visual itself is simple — the headline pull-quote does the work. The image has to look like a real person, not a stock-photo woman.

```
Photograph of an Australian woman in her late 30s or early 40s, wearing a
casual but put-together top — soft grey or cream t-shirt, light cotton blouse,
or simple linen shirt. Hair natural, minimal makeup. She is sitting at her
[kitchen island / dining table / on her couch in a sunlit lounge room] with
her hands relaxed in her lap or holding a mug of tea. Looking directly at the
camera with a calm, warm, closed-mouth smile — she looks happy but not
performative. Soft natural daylight from a large window to the side, neutral
colour temperature. Background is a gently blurred Australian home interior
with neutral tones, perhaps a houseplant or a child's drawing pinned softly
out of focus behind her. Natural skin texture, visible fine lines around the
eyes, no retouching whatsoever. Shot from chest-up, eye level. Looks like a
real photograph of a real mum, NOT a portrait session, NOT a stock image,
NOT a magazine cover.
```

**Then append the negative block.**

The headline pull-quote is overlaid in code afterwards, in Tutero serif or sans, beside her on the right (if she's facing left) or left (if she's facing right). The quote should describe a *non-grade* outcome: confidence, screen time, home harmony, the child's mood.

## Composition 5 — Polaroid Trio Collage

**Source:** Winner 11
**Winning moves:** 2 (faces × 3), 5 (Australianness), 7 (volume of evidence as soft persona variety)
**Mode:** Warm Staged
**Best for:** Mid-funnel statics, "real people use this" proof
**Aspect ratio:** 1:1 (the collage is square; each individual photo is generated then composited)

Three real-feel photos arranged as tilted polaroids. Volume of evidence: one photo is an anecdote, three is a pattern. Each photo should be a different family, different room, different angle — but the same flat aesthetic and same Australianness signals.

**Generate THREE separate photos using this base template, varying the persona each time:**

```
Photograph of [persona variant] sitting at a [varying surface] in their
Australian home, working together on a laptop showing a Tutero tutoring session
in progress. [Specific moment — e.g. mum pointing at the screen, child laughing
at something the tutor said, both leaning in to see better]. Flat overhead
household lighting, neutral colour temperature. Real Australian suburban home
interior — visible details like [specific AU details that vary per photo: a
fridge magnet, a school certificate on the wall, a fruit bowl, a footy ball
in the corner]. Looks like a phone snapshot a parent took and texted to a
friend.
```

**Three persona variants to use across the trio:**
1. Mum + primary-age daughter, kitchen counter
2. Dad + teen son, dining table
3. Mum + two siblings huddled around one laptop, family room couch

**Then append the negative block to each.**

Composite the three images in HTML/CSS as tilted polaroids:
- Slight rotation per photo (-4°, +2°, -2°)
- Subtle drop shadow
- White polaroid border (8-12% of photo width)
- Overlapping but each face fully visible
- Soft cream or pale background behind the collage

Add 2-3 ticked benefits below the collage in Tutero brand. The risk-reducer tick goes second.

## Composition 6 — Teen Self-Review (Bedroom UGC Still)

**Source:** Winner 8
**Winning moves:** 2 (face), 4 (outcome substitution — sells the teen's experience, not grades), 7 (persona specificity — teen)
**Mode:** Warm Staged for the look-at-camera; the framing is intentionally amateur
**Best for:** Teen-mum persona targeting, video thumbnails, UGC-style stills
**Aspect ratio:** 9:16 (vertical) or 4:5

The teen-in-bedroom UGC frame is the highest-trust UGC setup on Meta because it bypasses "the parent was paid to say this". The frame must look like the teen filmed herself on her own phone propped against a stack of books.

```
Phone selfie video still of an Australian teenage girl, age 15-17, sitting on
her bed in her bedroom. She is looking directly into the camera (slightly above
eye level — phone propped on books or held up). Casual home clothes —
oversized t-shirt, hoodie, or school uniform polo. Natural hair, minimal or no
makeup. Behind her: a typical teen girl's bedroom with personal touches —
fairy lights, posters, a few stuffed animals on a shelf, a desk visible in the
corner with a laptop and notebooks. Soft window light from one side combined
with the warm bedroom ceiling light. She has a natural, mid-sentence
expression — caught between thoughts, slight smile, eyes engaged. Looks
exactly like a TikTok or Instagram Reel still that she filmed herself.
Slightly soft focus, slight phone-camera grain, vertical orientation.
```

**Then append the negative block — but for this composition ALSO add to the negative:**
`professional video, filmed by someone else, ring light, cleaned-up bedroom,
styled background, perfect makeup, influencer aesthetic`

**Variant:** Same setup but boy in a teen boy's bedroom. Less common, higher persona-specificity for parents of teen sons.

## Universal append-block

After every composition prompt, append this block (also in `aesthetic-rules.md` in full):

```
NEGATIVE / AVOID: AI glow, soft rim lighting around subjects, golden hour orange,
teal and orange grading, dreamy bokeh, smooth airbrushed skin, perfect symmetrical
composition, cinematic colour science, HDR look, over-saturated warm peachy skin
tones, studio lighting, fashion editorial aesthetic, Pinterest mood board feel,
generic diverse stock group arrangement, apple on desk, chalkboard, graduation
cap, headset on child, corporate tutoring centre interior, text or watermark in
the image, brand logos, perfect tidy environments.

POSITIVE EMPHASIS: Flat household lighting. Neutral white balance. Natural skin
texture with visible pores and small imperfections. Real Australian suburban
home. Looks like an iPhone photo, not a stylised AI render.
```
