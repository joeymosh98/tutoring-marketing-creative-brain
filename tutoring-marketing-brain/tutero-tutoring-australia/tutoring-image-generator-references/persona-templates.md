# Persona Templates

Five named-persona prompt skeletons for AU paid social. These pair with the compositions in `au-winning-compositions.md` — pick a composition (the visual structure), pick a persona (who's in it), then fill in the prompt.

The reason to name a persona: "for everyone" sells to no one. When the parent in the image clearly *is* the parent watching the ad, identification is instant. This is Move 7 from `paid-social-winner-insights-australia`.

---

## Persona 1 — Jess, the Working Mum

**Source:** Winner 9
**Sells:** Guilt relief. Convenience. "I can't be everywhere."
**Best compositions:** School Gate Hug, Whole Transaction in One Frame
**Headline pattern:** "Jess works full-time and uses Tutero for weekly tutoring"

**Visual variables to fill in:**
- Age: 35–42
- Clothing: smart casual work — blazer over t-shirt, professional dress, lanyard sometimes visible
- Setting: school gate, kitchen counter on a weekday morning, or coming home through the front door with a laptop bag still on her shoulder
- Child: primary-school age (6–11), in school uniform
- Emotional beat: the moment she relaxes because she knows her kid has tutoring sorted
- AU signals: school uniform, AU public school setting, Aussie suburban home

**Prompt fragment to drop into a composition template:**
```
[Jess persona] An Australian working mum, age 38, wearing a navy blazer over
a soft grey t-shirt, hair in a low ponytail, looking slightly tired but warm
and present. Her [9-year-old daughter / 8-year-old son] is in a typical
Australian primary school uniform — white polo with school crest, navy and
green checked summer dress / navy shorts, school bag still on shoulders.
```

## Persona 2 — Em, the ATAR-Stressed Teen Parent

**Sells:** Performance under pressure. Confidence in the lead-up to exams. Reducing the household stress that comes with Year 11–12.
**Best compositions:** Whole Transaction in One Frame (with teen instead of primary kid), Testimonial Portrait
**Headline pattern:** "Em's son is in Year 12 and needed an ATAR-ready maths tutor — fast"

**Visual variables:**
- Parent age: 42–50
- Clothing: casual home — soft jumper, comfortable shirt, no work clothes
- Teen: 16–18, school uniform OR casual home clothes (hoodie, school polo untucked)
- Setting: dining table or teen's bedroom desk, evening
- Emotional beat: parent and teen actually getting along — the rare moment that tutoring is making household life easier, not adding pressure
- AU signals: Year 11/12 textbook visible, ATAR/HSC/VCE study notes, AU school uniform

**Prompt fragment:**
```
[Em persona] An Australian mum in her late 40s, wearing a soft cream cardigan
over a t-shirt, sitting beside her 17-year-old son at the family dining table.
The son is in his school uniform (white shirt, school tie loosened, navy
trousers) — clearly home from a Year 12 school day. A Year 12 maths textbook
and a workbook are open beside the laptop. Both look engaged but relaxed.
The mum is leaning slightly toward the screen, the son is pointing at
something on the laptop.
```

## Persona 3 — Sarah, the ADHD / Learning-Difference Parent

**Sells:** Patience. 1:1 attention. The fact that the child won't be left behind or made to feel stupid.
**Best compositions:** Whole Transaction in One Frame, Testimonial Portrait
**Headline pattern:** "Sarah's son has ADHD and needed a tutor who actually got him"

**This persona requires extra care.** The image must NEVER signal pity, struggle, or difference in a way that could feel exploitative. The child should look engaged and happy — the *parent* in the testimonial copy carries the weight of the ADHD/LD framing, not the visual.

**Visual variables:**
- Parent age: 35–45
- Clothing: casual home, slightly more relaxed than Jess
- Child: 8–12, in casual home clothes (NOT school uniform — implies a more flexible learning approach)
- Setting: family room, kitchen, or a calm corner of the home with soft furnishings
- Emotional beat: child is actually engaged with the tutor on screen, not zoning out — this is the visual proof
- AU signals: Aussie home, AU vernacular on any visible worksheet

**Prompt fragment:**
```
[Sarah persona] An Australian mum in her late 30s wearing a comfortable
oversized linen shirt, sitting beside her 10-year-old son on a couch with a
laptop on a coffee table. The son is wearing a casual t-shirt and shorts,
fully focused on the friendly tutor on the screen, leaning in with curiosity.
The mum has her hand resting gently on his back but is letting him drive the
session. Soft cushions, a few books and a fidget toy on the coffee table.
```

**Avoid in the image:** anything that visually flags the child as "different" or "struggling". The persona is named in the headline copy — the visual is just a happy kid getting good 1:1 attention.

## Persona 4 — The Regional Family

**Sells:** Access. "Same quality as a Sydney/Melbourne kid". No driving 90 minutes for a tutor. Local schools don't have advanced options.
**Best compositions:** Whole Transaction in One Frame, Polaroid Trio (one of the three should be a regional family)
**Headline pattern:** "Living regional doesn't mean limited tutoring options"

**Visual variables:**
- Parent age: 30–45
- Clothing: practical, regional Australian — bootcut jeans, work shirt, riding boots, sometimes a Akubra-adjacent hat hanging on a hook in the background
- Setting: a more rural-coded Australian home — wooden farmhouse kitchen, country veranda visible through a window, paddock outside
- Child: any age, often in a regional/rural school uniform (these tend to be more bottle-green or maroon than suburban schools)
- Emotional beat: the parent's quiet pride that their kid has access
- AU signals: this is the strongest possible AU signal — visibly regional Australia

**Prompt fragment:**
```
[Regional family persona] An Australian dad in his early 40s wearing a navy
work shirt with the sleeves rolled up, sitting at a worn timber kitchen table
in a rural Australian farmhouse. Beside him, his 11-year-old daughter in a
maroon and gold regional primary school uniform. A laptop is open between
them showing a tutoring session. Through the kitchen window behind them,
visible: gum trees, dry grass, a wide blue sky — unmistakably rural Australia.
A pair of work boots by the back door. The dad has a quiet, proud expression;
the daughter is leaning in, focused on the tutor.
```

## Persona 5 — The Switcher (post-Cluey, post-tutoring-centre)

**Sells:** "We tried the others and they didn't work. Tutero is different." This is the highest-intent persona — they've already paid for tutoring elsewhere.
**Best compositions:** Whole Transaction in One Frame, Testimonial Portrait
**Headline pattern:** "We tried [generic tutoring centre], it didn't stick. Then we found Tutero."

**Visual rule:** never name a competitor in the image. Keep the visual clean and positive. The competitor framing lives in the headline copy only.

**Visual variables:**
- Parent age: 35–45
- Mood: relieved, slightly amused, the body language of someone who's been through a few attempts and finally found the one that works
- Setting: kitchen or family room, the most "normal" possible Australian home — this persona converts on the *comfort* of finding the right thing
- Child: any age, visibly engaged
- AU signals: standard suburban AU home

**Prompt fragment:**
```
[Switcher persona] An Australian mum in her early 40s, wearing a soft striped
t-shirt and jeans, sitting at the kitchen counter with her 12-year-old daughter.
A laptop between them shows a tutoring session in progress. The daughter is
fully engaged with the screen. The mum is watching her daughter rather than
the screen, with a small relieved smile — the body language of "we finally
found one that works". Standard suburban Australian kitchen behind them.
```

## How to use a persona with a composition

```
1. Pick a composition from au-winning-compositions.md (e.g. "Whole Transaction in One Frame")
2. Pick a persona from this file (e.g. "Jess, the Working Mum")
3. Drop the persona's prompt fragment into the composition's [parent persona] / [child] slots
4. Append the universal negative/positive block from aesthetic-rules.md
5. Render via Nano Banana 2 at the right aspect ratio
6. View, score against the checklist, regenerate if needed
```
