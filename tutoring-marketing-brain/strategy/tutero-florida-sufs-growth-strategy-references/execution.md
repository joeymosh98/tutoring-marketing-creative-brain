# Tutero Florida — Execution Playbook

## Next 10 Steps (Sequenced, 8-Week Timeline)

Sequence matters more than speed. Steps 1–3 are the foundation. Steps 4–6 build the engine. Steps 7–10 turn it on.

### Step 1: Lock the Brand Language
**Owner:** Joey | **Deadline:** Week 1
**Deliverable:** 1-page "Tutero Florida Brand Language Guide" distributed to every team member.
Extract the language table, voice principles, and scholarship language hierarchy into a single-page reference. Pin it. Make it the first thing discussed in the next team meeting. Get the language right and every subsequent step benefits.

### Step 2: Build Three Segment Landing Pages
**Owner:** Joey (build) + Lauren/Sarah (copy review) | **Deadline:** Week 2
**Deliverable:** 3 live landing pages on Tutero.com — UA families, PEP/homeschool families, "New to Step Up" families.
Each page has one form, one CTA, and no navigation links that let visitors leave without converting. Match landing page headlines to ad headlines.

### Step 3: Record the First Content Batch
**Owner:** Tara (coordinate/edit) + Lauren, Sarah, Dani (record) | **Deadline:** Week 2
**Deliverable:** 10 short videos (60 seconds each), edited and captioned.
Schedule a single 90-minute block with each AM. Tara prepares topic list from the 30 post concepts. Each person records 3–4 clips. Tara edits within the week. Creates two-week content runway and establishes the recording habit.

### Step 4: Launch Organic Social Accounts
**Owner:** Tara | **Deadline:** Week 2 (accounts live), Week 3 (first posts)
**Deliverable:** @tuteroflorida live on Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook with bios and first 5 posts scheduled.
One post per day, five days per week. First 30 days = establish cadence and learn what resonates, not go viral.

### Step 5: Launch the Facebook Group
**Owner:** Joey (create) + Dani (daily management) + Lauren/Sarah (expert content) | **Deadline:** Week 3
**Deliverable:** "Step Up for Students Resources — Florida Families" live, with description, rules, 3 pinned posts, 20+ seed members.
Pinned posts: (1) welcome, (2) "Step Up Scholarships 101" guide, (3) "Post your questions here" thread. Joey runs $200 Meta ad promoting the group. Target: 50 members by end of week 4.

### Step 6: Launch First Three Meta Ad Campaigns
**Owner:** Joey | **Deadline:** Week 3
**Deliverable:** 3 live campaigns (UA/IEP, PEP/homeschool, retargeting), each pointing to correct segment landing page.
UA: Ads 1, 3, 6. PEP: Ads 4, 5. Retargeting: Ad 7. Each campaign has 2–3 variants for testing. 2-week test budget, then review with CPES metric and reallocate.

### Step 7: Build the Pediatrician Outreach List
**Owner:** Dani (research) + Lauren/Sarah (outreach) | **Deadline:** Week 4
**Deliverable:** 40 target practices across Miami, Tampa, Orlando, Jacksonville, tracked in Notion.
Capture: practice name, lead contact, email, phone, specialty, any connection to existing families. Prioritize ADHD, learning disabilities, autism spectrum specialties.

### Step 8: Create the Referral Materials Kit
**Owner:** Joey (design/copy) + Tara (production) | **Deadline:** Week 4
**Deliverable:** One-page Tutero overview PDF, Step Up resource guide PDF, referral cards (digital + print-ready), shareable family referral link template.
These must exist before any outreach happens. They are the handshake that opens the relationship.

### Step 9: Begin Pediatrician Outreach
**Owner:** Lauren/Sarah (outreach) + Dani (tracking/follow-up) | **Deadline:** Week 5–8 (ongoing)
**Deliverable:** Introductory emails to first 20 practices, 5+ meetings scheduled, referral materials delivered to 3+ offices.
Introductory email: professional, brief. Who they are by background, what Tutero does, offer to provide free scholarship resource materials. No ask for a referral partnership — just helpfulness. Dani tracks everything in Notion.

### Step 10: Run the First Monthly Review
**Owner:** Joey | **Deadline:** Week 8
**Deliverable:** 60-minute team meeting reviewing all 5 metrics + written 1-page summary of what's working, what's not, 3 priorities for next 30 days.
Pull numbers: active students, CPES by channel, referral rate, qualified leads/week. Review content and ad performance. Review Facebook group and outreach progress. Three questions: (1) What's working better than expected? Do more. (2) What's underperforming? Diagnose. (3) What to start, stop, or change?

## Content Flywheel

### Four Stages

1. **Creation:** Tara = 1 polished post/day (5/week batched). Lauren, Sarah, Dani = 1 expert video each/week (60 seconds). Joey = 1 strategic piece/month.
2. **Distribution:** Every core asset → 3+ channels. A Lauren video → Reel + TikTok + Facebook post + group clip + quote graphic. A scholarship guide → carousel + blog + group pin + email nurture.
3. **Engagement:** Track which topics/formats/angles drive most engagement weekly. Feed insights back into creation.
4. **Recycling:** High-performing content repurposed every 8–12 weeks with fresh creative. Evergreen scholarship content re-shared before every deadline.

### Systematizing Production

Tara owns daily social. AMs commit to one 15-min recording/week — Tara handles editing/distribution. Joey reviews calendar weekly, provides strategic direction, does not produce daily content.

Build Notion repository: Ideas (tagged by pillar/audience), In Production (assigned with deadlines), Published (with performance data for recycling). Every team meeting includes 10-min content review.

### Testimonial Capture

At 3-month mark, AM checks in with every family. "Would you be comfortable sharing your experience?" If yes, Tara follows up with simple video recording guide.

**Sensitivity protocol:** Families approve final edits. Children never identified by full name. Diagnoses mentioned only if family includes them. Tone always hopeful, never exploitative. Family can withdraw at any time.

## Measurement Framework

### The 5 Metrics That Matter

| # | Metric | What It Measures | Track |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Active Students** | Students with 1+ lesson this month | Weekly |
| 2 | **Cost Per Enrolled Student (CPES)** | Total marketing spend ÷ new students who start | Monthly by channel |
| 3 | **Referral Rate** | % new students from existing family referrals | Monthly |
| 4 | **Retention Rate** | % students still active after 6 months | Monthly (from month 6) |
| 5 | **Qualified Leads/Week** | Families contacted with correct scholarship + genuine intent | Weekly by channel |

### Targets

| Metric | 30 Days | 90 Days | 180 Days | 365 Days | 18 Months |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Active Students | 30 | 100 | 250 | 600 | 1,200+ |
| CPES (Meta) | $400 | $300 | $250 | $200 | $150 |
| Referral Rate | 5% | 10% | 20% | 30% | 35%+ |
| 6-Mo Retention | — | — | 75% | 80% | 85% |
| Qualified Leads/Wk | 8 | 15 | 25 | 40 | 60+ |

### Decision Triggers

- **Double down:** CPES below target + qualified leads above target for 2 consecutive months.
- **Investigate:** CPES rises above target for 2 months. Diagnose: creative fatigue, audience saturation, or funnel leakage.
- **Pivot:** No improvement after 3 months of systematic testing. Reallocate to highest-performing channel.
- **Celebrate:** Referral rate exceeds 25%. Shift investment from paid → community and content.
