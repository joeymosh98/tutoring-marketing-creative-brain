# tutero.ai — Marketing Strategy & Positioning

**Confidential — March 2026**

Always read this skill before writing any tutero.ai marketing copy, ad creative, social content, or go-to-market material. For visual brand identity (colours, typography, logos, hex codes), also read the `tutero-brand` skill.

## Critical Context

- The product goes to market as **tutero.ai** — NOT "Tutero Studio" or "Tutero Schools"
- Multi-subject is launching (expanding from maths into every subject)
- The company name is **Tutero** — tutero.ai is the product URL and how teachers refer to it
- This is a PLG business: free teachers → paid educators → school deals

## Positioning

### The Statement

tutero.ai is the AI teaching platform that creates curriculum-aligned resources for any subject, any year level, any student — in minutes. Built for teachers. Not adapted from ChatGPT.

### Category

**AI teaching platform.** Not "AI-powered tool", not "resource generator", not "EdTech platform." We are an AI company that built a teaching platform. The category we're building toward is "Intelligent Teaching Design" — but we don't use that phrase externally yet. The product earns it.

### North Star

The Canva for teaching. We don't say this publicly — we let people draw the conclusion themselves. The product's quality, design, and breadth should make this comparison inevitable.

### Multi-Subject Framing

"We started with maths because we wanted to get it right. Now tutero.ai works for every subject you teach."

This frames the expansion as intentional quality-first sequencing, not catching up. It implies: if we got maths this right, imagine what we'll do with your subject.

### Competitive Positioning (never name competitors in external copy)

| Competitor | Our angle | One-line reframe |
|---|---|---|
| ChatGPT / generic AI | Purpose-built vs adapted | "Built for teachers. Not adapted from ChatGPT." |
| TpT / resource banks | Create vs consume | "Someone else's idea of what your class needs." |
| MagicSchool / Diffit | Platform vs feature | We don't engage directly — we position above the category |
| Manual creation | Time vs quality | "You became a teacher to teach. Not to spend Sunday making worksheets." |

## Brand Voice

### Character

Confident, direct, slightly provocative. Not the warm colleague at the staff party — more like the teacher who built something genuinely better and isn't shy about saying so. Think: founder energy crossed with teacher credibility. Not arrogant. Not salesy. Just unapologetically good at what we do.

### Rules

- **Declarative, not descriptive.** "The AI teaching platform." Not "A platform that allows teachers to..."
- **Short sentences. Full stops.** Confidence lives in brevity.
- **Sentence case always.** Never Title Case headlines, never ALL CAPS (except "AI" and proper nouns).
- **No "AI-powered" anything.** We ARE an AI company. We don't bolt AI onto things.
- **No corporate softeners.** No "helps teachers", "enables educators", "empowers classrooms." Direct verbs: creates, builds, makes.
- **No exclamation marks in headlines.** The work speaks for itself.
- **"Free" is a brand statement, not just pricing.** "Free for every teacher. Seriously."

### Tone Spectrum

| Context | Tone |
|---|---|
| Landing page hero | Bold, declarative, confident |
| Product descriptions | Clear, specific, no jargon |
| Social media (provocative) | Opinionated, slightly irreverent |
| Social media (relatable) | Warm, human, "we get it" energy |
| Social media (demos) | Let the product speak — minimal narration |
| Founder content | Raw, authentic, mission-driven |
| Ad copy | Punchy, problem-aware, scroll-stopping |
| Investor framing | Ambitious, data-backed, category-defining |

## Audience Segments & Messaging

### Segment 1: Teachers Who Tried ChatGPT

**Insight:** They already believe AI should help them — they just haven't found the right tool.

**Hook:** The ChatGPT formatting frustration. They know exactly what it feels like to get raw text and spend 45 minutes making it usable.

**Key messages:**
- "Built for teachers. Not adapted from ChatGPT."
- "You had the right instinct. AI should do this."
- The visual comparison (ChatGPT raw text vs Tutero formatted resource) is the most persuasive asset for this audience.

### Segment 2: Time-Poor Teachers

**Insight:** They're drowning in prep work. Sunday nights, late nights, the constant grind of making resources.

**Hook:** Name the specific time cost. "Sunday, 10pm" is universal. "47 seconds" is the proof.

**Key messages:**
- "You became a teacher to teach. Not to spend Sunday making worksheets."
- "47 seconds. That's how long it takes."
- "Get your Sundays back."

### Segment 3: Teachers Discovering AI

**Insight:** They haven't tried AI for teaching yet. They need to see what's possible, not be sold on switching.

**Hook:** Show breadth and possibility. "Every resource you can imagine" + real examples across subjects.

**Key messages:**
- "Every resource you can imagine."
- "Now available for every subject."
- "What would you teach if creating resources was effortless?"

### Segment 4: School Leaders (HODs, Principals, District)

**Hook:** Their teachers are already using it. The school deal gives visibility and control.

**Key messages:**
- "Your teachers are already spending hours on resource creation. Give them professional-grade AI tools."
- "Free tier means zero-risk adoption."
- Proof: teacher retention, satisfaction, consistency across departments.

## PLG (Product-Led Growth) Mechanics

### The Core Funnel

```
Free teacher sign-up → Activated (creates first resource) → Retained (active week 4+) → School cluster (3+ teachers at same school) → School deal
```

### Key Metrics

- **Cost Per Activated Teacher (CPAT)** — not cost per sign-up. A sign-up who never creates has zero value.
- **Viral coefficient** — how many additional teachers each activated teacher brings in organically.
- **School cluster rate** — % of activated teachers who end up at a school with 3+ active Tutero teachers.
- **Activation rate** — % of sign-ups who create a resource within 7 days. THE most important metric.

### PLG Principles

- The resource IS the distribution. Every PDF, every shared link, every template = brand impression.
- Free tier must be genuinely generous. Gate the insights layer, not the creation.
- "3+ teachers at same school" is the sales trigger. Bottom-up adoption creates top-down deals.
- The landing page hero IS the product experience (interactive creation form, not a brochure).

## Social Content Strategy

### Account: Tutero-Teachers

### Platforms: TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts

### Cadence: 5 posts/week (weekdays)

### Content Pillars

| Pillar | Mix | What it is | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Watch This | 40–50% | Screen recordings of resources being created. Speed is the hook, quality is the proof. 30–60 sec. | Drive sign-ups through product demonstration |
| Why Does This Exist | 20–25% | Provocative takes on teaching tools. Opinions, challenges to status quo. | Build brand as a voice, drive engagement |
| Teacher Reality | 15–20% | Relatable teacher experience content. POV, memes, trending audio. | Build following through shared experience |
| Behind the Scenes | 5–10% | Founder story, team content, building in public. | Humanise the brand |
| Creator Collab | 5–10% | Real teachers using tutero.ai on camera. | Social proof through credibility |

### Platform Nuances

- **TikTok:** Pillar 2 takes + Pillar 3 relatable. Personality and opinion first.
- **IG Reels:** Pillar 1 demos + polished Pillar 3. Visual quality matters most.
- **YT Shorts:** Pillar 1 with more detail. YouTube tolerates explanation.

### Video Rules

- Hook in first 2 seconds. Always.
- Screen recordings: clean desktop, no browser chrome, timer overlay for speed demos, hold on finished resource 3–5 seconds.
- On-camera: natural lighting, conversational energy (not presenter), clean background.
- Creator content: send brief, let them react naturally. Authenticity > polish.
- No intro logos or bumpers. Start with the content.

## Objection Handling

Use these reframes in copy, social replies, ad captions, and sales conversations:

| Objection | Reframe |
|---|---|
| "I already use ChatGPT" | ChatGPT gives you text. We give you print-ready, differentiated, curriculum-aligned resources. Try making the same thing in both. |
| "I don't trust AI quality" | Good — that's why Edit with AI exists. You're always in control. The AI drafts, you decide. |
| "My school won't pay" | It's free for individual teachers. When your department sees the quality, the school conversation happens naturally. |
| "No time to learn new tools" | First resource takes 90 seconds. If you can describe a lesson to a colleague, you can use tutero.ai. |
| "It's just for maths" | Not anymore. Every subject, every resource type, same quality. |
| "How is this different from MagicSchool?" | Most AI tools give you templates. We give you a creation platform. The outputs look like they came from the best department in your school. |
