## Company Positioning

Tutero is an **educator enablement company**.

That means: Tutero builds software that makes educators more effective. Not software that replaces teachers. The product helps educators -both classroom teachers and 1-on-1 tutors -evaluate their students, structure learning pathways, and deliver high-efficacy teaching with AI-generated resources.

**One-line positioning:** "Software that helps educators evaluate, plan, and teach -powered by AI."

This positioning sits above both sub-brands. Whether the audience is a school principal, an investor, or a parent, the underlying truth is the same: Tutero makes great teaching possible. But how you express it changes dramatically by audience (see Messaging by Audience below).

## The Diagnosis-to-Mastery Framework

This is Tutero's core approach to teaching and learning. It describes how the product works and why it's effective.

### The four stages

| Stage | What happens | Audience-friendly language |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Diagnosis** | Diagnostic testing evaluates where each student is right now -knowledge gaps, strengths, readiness | "We start by understanding exactly where your student is" |
| **2. Curriculum alignment** | Results are mapped against the relevant curriculum to identify what needs to be learned next | "Then we map a path to where they need to be" |
| **3. Custom resources** | AI generates tailored teaching materials -worksheets, explanations, practice problems -matched to each student's level | "We create resources built for them, not a textbook average" |
| **4. Personalisation** | An adaptive learning pathway is built so every student progresses at the right pace toward mastery | "Every lesson moves them forward" |

### Where to use this framework -and where NOT to

| Context | Use diagnosis-to-mastery? | How |
|---|---|---|
| **Website "How it works"** | Yes | Full four-stage narrative |
| **Pitch decks / investor materials** | Yes | Present as a product flywheel |
| **tutero.ai / Schools copy** | Yes | Lead with "curriculum alignment" and "custom resources" -teachers care about alignment and time saved |
| **Internal docs** | Yes | Full framework, technical language fine |
| **Parent-facing tutoring copy** | **No -not directly** | Parents never see the framework, AI, or technical process. Instead, the benefits surface through the five pillars (see below): "your tutor already knows where your child is stuck" (Knowing), "we find and fix gaps in knowledge" (Proof). The mechanism is invisible; the outcome is what parents see. |
| **Parent-facing tutoring ads** | **No** | AI and process language are hard-banned in parent-facing copy. See Zara's parent-copy skill for the complete forbidden words list. |

## The Five Pillars (Tutoring)

These are the structural messaging framework for all parent-facing tutoring copy. Every long-form piece stacks them in order. Short-form copy picks one and goes deep. The order never changes.

1. **The Faculty.** Top 1.3% acceptance rate. Vetted and verified. Custom tutor pairing -matched by a real person, not an algorithm.
2. **The Knowing.** Your tutor walks into Tuesday's lesson already knowing where your child is stuck. We find and fix gaps in knowledge. Weekly lesson reports so you always know what's happening.
3. **The Proof.** A written lesson report after every session. Watch your child's confidence grow. Improve grades. See progress after 3 lessons.
4. **The Open Door.** No contracts. No upfront fees. Cancel anytime. Money back guarantee. Free tutoring consultation to get started.
5. **The Account Manager.** A real person assigned from day one. Matches your tutor. Reads every lesson report. Picks up the phone. This is the white-glove pillar -it must appear in any copy that addresses trust or process.

### The proof stack -canonical numbers

Use these exact numbers in tutoring copy. Never invent. Never approximate upward.

| Proof point | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Top 1.3%** acceptance rate | The most powerful single number in the brand |
| **30,000+ applications per year** | Backs the 1.3%. Use when 1.3% needs grounding |
| **4,000+ active Australian families** | Social proof |
| **200,000+ lessons delivered** | Scale |
| **3,000+ hours of tutoring every week** | Momentum |
| **98.1% of students** report improved confidence | The confidence pillar's proof |
| **Vetted, verified, and background-checked** | Faculty rigour -use when the parent's worry is safety |
| **Lesson report after every session** | The Proof pillar -say it exactly this way |
| **No contracts, no upfront fees** | The Open Door -say it exactly this way |
| **Personal account manager** | The white-glove pillar |
| **Money back guarantee** | Risk elimination |

If a brief asks for a stat that isn't on this list, stop and ask. Don't fabricate.

## Messaging by Audience

### Teachers (tutero.ai)

Teachers are time-poor, sceptical of ed-tech promises, and protective of their professional judgement. Speak to them as peers, not customers.

**tutero.ai brand voice (distinct from company and tutoring):** Confident, direct, slightly provocative. Not the warm colleague at the staff party -more like the teacher who built something genuinely better and isn't shy about saying so. Founder energy crossed with teacher credibility.

**Voice rules:**
- **Declarative, not descriptive.** "The AI teaching platform." Not "A platform that allows teachers to..."
- **Short sentences. Full stops.** Confidence lives in brevity.
- **Sentence case always.** Never Title Case headlines, never ALL CAPS (except "AI" and proper nouns).
- **No corporate softeners.** No "helps teachers", "enables educators", "empowers classrooms." Direct verbs: creates, builds, makes.
- **No exclamation marks in headlines.** The work speaks for itself.
- **"Free" is a brand statement, not just pricing.** "Free for every teacher. Seriously."

**What they care about:**
- Saving preparation time (be specific: "47 seconds to create a resource", not "enhances efficiency")
- Curriculum alignment to their specific standards (Australian Curriculum, CCSS, state standards)
- Resources they can actually use tomorrow, not next term
- Maintaining their role -AI drafts, teacher decides
- Differentiation -resources that meet each student where they are, not one-size-fits-all

**Three teacher segments:**

| Segment | Insight | Hook |
|---|---|---|
| **Teachers who tried ChatGPT** | They already believe AI should help them -they just haven't found the right tool | The formatting frustration. "Stop reformatting ChatGPT output." The visual comparison (raw text vs Tutero resource) is the most persuasive asset. |
| **Time-poor teachers** | Drowning in prep. Sunday nights, late nights, the constant grind | Name the specific time cost. "Sunday, 10pm. Still making Monday's worksheets?" / "47 seconds." |
| **Teachers discovering AI** | Haven't tried AI for teaching yet. Need to see what's possible, not be sold on switching | Show breadth and possibility. "Every resource you can imagine." + real examples across subjects. |

**AI is visible here.** Teachers know and appreciate that tutero.ai is an AI tool. Lead with what the AI does for them (curriculum-aligned resources, differentiated materials, instant creation) -not AI as a buzzword.

**Avoid:** "Helps teachers" (too passive), vague promises, anything that implies the teacher is replaceable, corporate jargon, exclamation marks.

### School leaders (tutero.ai)

Principals and heads of curriculum care about two things above all: **increasing the productivity of their teachers** and **enabling differentiation in their classrooms**.

**What they care about:**
- **Teacher productivity** -reducing planning and resourcing time so teachers can focus on teaching
- **Differentiation** -every student getting resources matched to their level, not a single worksheet for the whole class
- Whole-school adoption -does it work across year levels and subjects?
- Data and reporting -can they see what's happening across classes?
- Teacher buy-in -will staff actually use it? (Free removes the cost barrier, but they need to see teachers wanting it)
- Curriculum compliance -does it align with mandated standards?

**Tone:** Professional but warm. Evidence-led. Confident without overselling. Speak to the principal's vision for their school, not just features.

**Key framing:** tutero.ai gives teachers superpowers -it doesn't add to their workload. A school leader's nightmare is buying software that sits unused. Show that tutero.ai is the tool teachers already want because it saves them real time.

**The bottom-up play:** "Your teachers are already spending hours on resource creation. Give them professional-grade AI tools." Free tier = zero-risk adoption. 3+ teachers at the same school is the signal that a school deal is ready.

**Objection handling for school leaders:**

| Objection | Reframe |
|---|---|
| "My school won't pay" | It's free for individual teachers. When your department sees the quality, the school conversation happens naturally. |
| "I don't trust AI quality" | Good -that's why Edit with AI exists. The teacher is always in control. The AI drafts, the teacher decides. |
| "No time to learn new tools" | First resource takes 90 seconds. If a teacher can describe a lesson to a colleague, they can use tutero.ai. |
| "It's just for maths" | Not anymore. Every subject, every resource type, same quality. |

### Parents (Tutero Tutoring)

Every Tutero parent is in a moment of quiet crisis. They Google late at night. They've been disappointed before. They need to be able to say to their partner "this one is different, and here's why."

**The emotional destination:** Confidence. Parents don't buy maths. They buy a child who feels capable again. Confidence is the destination of every piece of tutoring copy, even when it isn't named directly.

**What they care about:**
- Their specific child's needs (not generic tutoring)
- Tutor quality -vetted, verified, from the top 1.3% of applicants
- The relationship -same tutor every week, a partnership, someone who knows their child
- Flexibility -pay per lesson, no lock-in, cancel anytime
- Visible progress -lesson reports after every session, confidence growing
- The white-glove experience -a real person (account manager) picks up the phone

**What Tutero is to a parent:** A **service delivered by humans**. It is never an "app", a "platform", or a "tool". The relationship is the product. White-glove, personal, and human.

**Tone:** Warm, hopeful, parent-to-parent. Like a recommendation from a friend whose kid is thriving. Short sentences. Plain Australian English. Clear. Confident.

**Avoid:**
- Any mention of AI, algorithms, machine learning, software, technology, automation -hard ban, no exceptions
- "Teachers" as a blanket term -say **"tutors"**. Not all Tutero tutors are qualified teachers. "Tutor" is the safe, accurate default. "Teacher" is fine when referring to a specific person who IS a registered teacher.
- Hard sell or fear-based messaging ("your child is falling behind")
- Anything that sounds like a call centre or a tech company
- "Platform", "app", "tool" as product descriptors
- Grades as the lead -grades can appear, but confidence is always the primary outcome

**Trust is shown, not claimed.** Every piece of parent-facing copy demonstrates at least one of: the personal account manager, the lesson report, the 1.3% acceptance rate, the money back guarantee, or vetted & verified tutors. Trust is not asserted -it is evidenced.

## Locale Rules

Tutero operates in **Australia** (primary market) and the **United States** (Florida / SUFS).

### Spelling

| AU English | US English |
|---|---|
| personalised | personalized |
| colour | color |
| favourite | favorite |
| organisation | organization |
| programme (educational) | program |
| centre | center |
| behaviour | behavior |
| analyse | analyze |
| enrolment | enrollment |
| practise (verb) | practice |
| licence (noun) | license |
| maths | math |

### Rules

- **One locale per asset.** Decide the target market first. Never mix AU and US spelling in a single piece.
- **AU is the default.** When the audience is mixed, global, or unspecified (e.g. homepage, investor deck, internal docs), use Australian English. Tutero is an Australian company.
- **US for Florida/SUFS only.** Only use US English when the asset is explicitly for the American market.
- **Year levels vs grades:** AU uses "Year 1–12" or "Prep–Year 12". US uses "Kindergarten–12th grade" or "K–12". Match the market.
- **Currency:** AU uses AUD ($). US uses USD ($). Always specify when the number appears without context.

## Forbidden & Preferred Language

### Hard-banned in ALL parent-facing tutoring copy

These words and concepts are **never used** in any copy a parent will read -ads, landing pages, emails, SMS, social, captions, CTAs. No exceptions.

| Forbidden | Why |
|---|---|
| **AI** (and all variants: algorithm, machine learning, software, technology, smart, powered by, automated, intelligent) | To parents, Tutero is a tutoring service, not a tech company. AI is the mechanism, not the message. |
| **Platform** / **app** (as product descriptor) | Tutero is a service delivered by humans. Mentioning the "mobile app for parents" as a feature/tick is fine. |
| **Teachers** (as blanket term for all tutors) | Not all tutors are qualified teachers. "Tutor" is the default. |

### Dead language (all audiences)

| Forbidden | Why | Say instead |
|---|---|---|
| "AI-powered" anything | Redundant -Tutero IS an AI company | Just describe what the AI does |
| "Cutting-edge" / "revolutionary" / "game-changing" | Empty superlatives | Be specific about the benefit |
| "Platform" alone (without qualifier) | Too generic | "AI teaching platform" or "teaching platform" (Schools only) |
| "Tutoring company" | Mispositions the company | "tutoring service" (parent-facing) or "AI education company" (corporate) |
| "Tutero Schools" | Legacy name, rebranded | "tutero.ai" |
| "Enhance efficiency" | Vague corporate speak | Specific benefit: "saves 3 hours of planning per week" |
| "Leverage AI" | Corporate jargon | "uses AI to..." or just describe what it does |
| "Stakeholders" | Dehumanising | "teachers", "parents", "families", "school leaders" |
| "Solution" | Empty tech word | Name the actual product or feature |
| "Empower" | Overused in ed-tech | "helps", "enables", "gives teachers..." |
| "Unlock potential" | Empty | Describe the specific outcome |
| "Expert" (as a noun for tutors) | Vague | "top 1.3%" or name a credential |
| "Tailored" | Too vague | Describe what the customisation actually is |

### Defensive language (reframe, don't use directly)

| Avoid | Say instead |
|---|---|
| "Risk-free" | "No contracts" or "money back guarantee" or "cancel anytime" |
| "Trial" | "Your first lesson" or "free tutoring consultation" |
| "Free intro lesson / free session / free lesson" | Tutero does not offer free lessons. Use "free tutoring consultation" or "book your first lesson" |
| "Sign up" / "enrol" | "Book your first lesson" or "get started" |
| "Cheap" / "budget" | "Affordable pricing" (as a tick, not a headline) |

### Preferred language -examples

| Instead of... | Write... |
|---|---|
| "Our platform uses AI to enhance learning outcomes" | "Teachers use Tutero to build personalised lessons in minutes" |
| "AI-powered personalised learning solution" | "AI that plans lessons around each student" (Schools only) |
| "We empower educators with cutting-edge tools" | "We save teachers three hours a week on lesson planning" |
| "Leverage our technology to drive student outcomes" | "Students learn faster when every lesson starts where they are" |
| "Expert tutors powered by AI-driven lesson plans" | "Vetted & verified tutors from the top 1.3% of applicants" |

## Quick Checklist -Before Publishing Any Copy

- [ ] **Market decided:** AU or US? Spelling matches throughout?
- [ ] **Brand layer identified:** Company, tutero.ai, or Tutero Tutoring?
- [ ] **If parent-facing:** No AI, no platform, no app-as-product, no "teachers" as blanket term?
- [ ] **If parent-facing:** Confidence is the emotional destination, not grades?
- [ ] **If parent-facing:** At least one trust signal shown (account manager, lesson report, 1.3%, vetted tutors, money back guarantee)?
- [ ] **If parent-facing:** "Tutor" used as default noun (not "teacher")?
- [ ] **No forbidden language** -scanned for dead language across all audiences
- [ ] **Specific, not vague** -benefits are concrete (time saved, outcomes achieved), not abstract
- [ ] **Tone is right** -human, confident, warm, hopeful, swift
- [ ] **Product naming correct** -tutero.ai (not Tutero Schools), Tutero Tutoring / Tutero.com (not "the tutoring platform")
- [ ] **Tagline consistent** -"Where great teaching happens" at company level
